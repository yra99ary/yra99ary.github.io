Zion

Your all-in-one secure wallet on the blockchain.




OVERVIEW

A comprehensive dashboard to view and manage all digital asset on Zion is simple and delightful. It can also provide a unique alternative way to restore wallet from your trusted contacts.

The goal of this project is to come up with iterated solutions for user's pain points in current crypto service and lighten the burden of entering the crypto world for them.

The Problem

Most crypto wallets are in early phases of implementation and adoption, and there is still room for improvement when users use these apps. Sending, receiving and storing cryptocurrency can be complicated and confusing, particularly for beginners.

There are many problems to be solved in our first cryptocurrency product with built-in hardware wallet on the phone. Thus, I start to cooperate with the user research team by using design thinking to solve these problems we found and try to seek the opportunity of this product.






User Research

We invited ten cryptocurrency users who are 25 to 36 year old to participate in this in-depth interviews for planning and designing further blockchain products.

The research objective is to understand

• Cryptocurrency user behaviors and wallet usages
• Investment behaviors of digital assets
• Pain points of investment of cryptocurrency






Insights

OVERALL

We found out that most of the participants complained about the recovery key that helps the user to restore wallet; they think it’s too long to remember and easy to forget and lose when in need. Crypto asset trading is the most popular activity in the blockchain. Most user regard cryptocurrency as an investment method. They also believe that there is still room for UX improvement in current crypto service.

User Profile

Based on the finding from an in-depth interview, I designed a Persona to help the team more empathetic to identify which problems are the most important to work on solving first.



Then we classified and organized the pain points into similar categories by using affinity mapping. After discussion, we placed the highest priority pain points into the 2 x 2 matrix.



As shown in the 2 x 2 matrix, we can understand that the recovery key is the most important part that contains both the business value and user need. It’s also the critical problem that need to be clarified and solved for the following product design.

Note: Out of all the problems solved I would like to showcase the primary two problems of Recovery key and Sending cryptocurrency.






Problem solved: The recovery key is too easy to forget and lose when in need.

We design a new way called “Social Key Backup” to those who accidentally lost a wallet or forgot the recovery key, can still restore wallet through people they trust.

View the original video on official YouTube channel

Our idea came from the previous interview, some participants share with us; they heard someone splits the recovery key and keep the parts in different locations or trusted people. We found most participants prefer to keep recovery key in trusted people than places. They feel that keep it in people they know is more reliable and difficult to forget.



(BEFORE)

User can't get the information they need at a glance, including the trusted contact list and the backup status of the trusted contact.

And users feel confused about if the social backup is enabled or not, and the indicator should be more clear.





(AFTER)

I combined invitation and list of trusted contact into one page, and made the whole page more clear and readable by using indicators to aware of the current status of backup status.



The Solution

• Social Key Recovery allows users to choose three to five trusted contacts who can help recover their wallet. Once restored, users’ contacts can accept recovery requests to help you recover wallet.

• A status reminder can help users to check contacts from time to time to see if they're still available to help recover wallet.

• If users' trusted contacts copy of recovery data had been lost or corrupted, users could tap Resend by the contact's name to send another copy of recovery data.

• Allow users to update trusted contacts as circumstances require. This might occur if a contact's phone is lost or stolen, or the contact is no longer able to assist in the recovery process.






Problem solved: Sending cryptocurrency can be complicated and confusing

(BEFORE)

We found that users tend to paste address than enter it by themselves and they want to make a note for each transaction.



(AFTER)

I made some design improvement in the version 2, including added more ways to enter an address, emphasized the paste function to replace the traditional input field that user entered the address and added an optional input field for users who want to take a memo.





The Solution

• We decided to use the current design(scroll panel) which is also has a higher success rate and effective than the previous design.

• Provide more ways to enter an address and use interaction to make the transaction process more intuitive and easy to use.

• The transaction fee is adjustable for different needs.

TL;DR

Zion marks a brand new beginning for HTC, and it is the first built-in hardware wallet on the Android phone. We are dedicated to delivering the best user experience through design thinking to help the user to reduce the burden of entering cryptocurrency world and come up with iterated solutions for users’ pain points we found from user research.

In our future roadmap, we will keep refining and updating future versions of Zion to make it better after receiving more user data. Moreover, there will be some more advanced functions including a partnership with Opera to extend DApp experience, payment, instant exchange and access to wider blockchain marketplace.
